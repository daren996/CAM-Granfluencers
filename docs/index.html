Research Project

Granfluencers in Digital Media

This project uses computational methods to study granfluencers on social media. It examines their content, portrayal, and user interactions to better understand how they shape engagement, perception, and advertising outcomes.

Project Aim

Understanding how granfluencers influence digital communication

The study focuses on granfluencers on Instagram and asks how older creators present themselves, what kinds of content they publish, and how audiences respond to them. The goal is to connect large-scale social media evidence with questions of communication, persuasion, and platform visibility.

By combining structured collection, multimodal description, and computational analysis, the project investigates both creator behavior and audience reaction across content, engagement, and advertising-related signals.

Platform
Instagram posts, captions, images, metadata, and detailed comment threads.
Methods
NLP, topic modeling, LLM-based coding, and large-scale content analysis.
Outputs
Workflow docs, synced datasets, analysis views, and research-ready findings.

Research Scope

Core dimensions

  • Posts, captions, hashtags, time, likes, comments, shares, and creator reply rate.
  • Image-level detail including behaviors, colors, objects, backgrounds, body exposure, and facial expression.
  • Advertising indicators such as products, brands, ad-related content, and proportion of commercial posts.
  • Granfluencer profiles and detailed audience comments for user-response analysis.

Work Flow

From collection to interpretation

  1. Collect large-scale Instagram data related to granfluencers and their posts.
  2. Preprocess text, image descriptions, metadata, and interaction records.
  3. Apply computational methods to code content features and media portrayal.
  4. Interpret results to support analysis, discussion, and potential conclusions.
